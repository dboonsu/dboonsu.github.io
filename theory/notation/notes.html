Notes represent a pitch and duration of a sound.



12-Tone Music

Mathematically, a note corresponds to a frequency measured in hertz (Hz) or vibrations per second. In concerts, orchestras will tune their instruments to a central note of A vibrating at 440 Hz. Suppose you doubled this note's frequency to 880 Hz. The resulting note would still be an A note at a higher pitch. Similarly, if you halved its frequency to 220 Hz, the result would be a lower pitched A. This rule of doubling and halving holds for any note.
